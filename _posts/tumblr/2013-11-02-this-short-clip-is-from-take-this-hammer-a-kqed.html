This short clip is from Take This Hammer, a KQED documentary from 1963 that follows James Baldwin on a trip to San Francisco where he speaks to members of the black community about the prevalence of racism in a city known for its liberalism. The full documentary is here.

Here in Seattle, a city also well known for its pioneering liberalism, our own public radio station just concluded a series on being black in Seattle. The series hits on a lot of the same issues as Take This Hammer, detailing how black Seattleites face gentrification, police discrimination, and social discrimination, but also explores how difficult it is for some black Seattleites just to establish connections with other members of the small and shrinking black population.
